Why Convert Word to PDF?

Converting Word documents to PDF is essential for sharing professional documents that preserve their formatting across all devices. PDFs are universally compatible, secure, and maintain the exact layout, fonts, and images from your original document.

Key Benefits of PDF Format

  • Universal Compatibility: PDFs look the same on any device, operating system, or browser
  • Preserved Formatting: Fonts, spacing, images, and layout stay exactly as intended
  • Security: PDFs are harder to edit without permission
  • Professional Appearance: PDFs look more polished for business communications
  • Compact File Size: PDFs are often smaller than original Word files
  • Print Ready: Perfect for professional printing

Understanding Font Compatibility

Standard Fonts (Best for PDF)

These fonts are universally available and will always render correctly: Arial, Times New Roman, Calibri, Helvetica, Courier, Georgia, Verdana, Tahoma.

Custom Fonts

If your Word document uses custom or uncommon fonts, the PDF converter will substitute them with similar standard fonts. This may slightly alter the appearance.

Common Issues and Solutions

My formatting looks different in the PDF

This usually happens with custom fonts, complex tables, or unusual layouts. Try using standard fonts and simplifying complex layouts before converting. For complex documents, consider adjusting margins or page breaks.

The file is too large

Use our free PDF compressor after conversion to reduce file size without losing quality.

Images are blurry

Make sure your original Word document uses high-resolution images (at least 150 DPI for screen viewing, 300 DPI for printing).

Page breaks are different

Word and PDF handle page breaks slightly differently. Check your document in Print Preview before converting to see how pages will break.

When to Use Word to PDF Conversion

Business Proposals

Convert business proposals and client documents to PDF to ensure they look professional and cannot be accidentally modified by recipients.

Resumes and Cover Letters

Always send resumes as PDFs to ensure employers see your formatting exactly as you intended, regardless of their computer setup.

Legal Documents

Contracts, agreements, and legal notices should be distributed as PDFs to prevent unauthorized modifications.

Academic Papers

Research papers, theses, and assignments submitted as PDFs maintain consistent formatting across different computers and operating systems.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the converter preserve tables and charts?

Yes! All tables, charts, graphs, and SmartArt are preserved during conversion. Complex layouts may be simplified for PDF compatibility.

What Word formats are supported?

We support DOCX files (Microsoft Word 2007 and newer). For older DOC files, open them in Word and save as DOCX before converting.

Is there a file size limit?

You can convert Word documents up to 50MB. For larger files, compress images in your Word document before converting.

Will hyperlinks work in the PDF?

Yes, hyperlinks are preserved in the converted PDF. Recipients can click links as they would in the original document.

Can I convert password-protected Word files?

You'll need to remove the password protection from your Word document before converting. Our tool doesn't support password-protected files.
