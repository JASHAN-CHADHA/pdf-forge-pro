Convert JPG to PDF: The Ultimate Step-by-Step Tutorial

Published: May 27, 2025 | 7 min read | By PDFForge Team

Why Convert JPG to PDF?

Converting images to PDF format is essential for many professional and personal tasks. PDFs are universally compatible, maintain consistent formatting across devices, and are more secure than individual image files. Whether you're creating a portfolio, submitting scanned documents, or organizing photos, PDF is the preferred format.

Common Reasons to Convert JPG to PDF

  • Document submission: Many institutions require PDF format for applications
  • Preserve quality: Maintain image resolution without compression artifacts
  • Combine multiple images: Create a single document from several photos
  • Professional presentations: Create polished portfolios and reports
  • Scanned documents: Convert scanned paperwork to searchable PDFs
  • Print ready format: Ensure consistent printing across different printers

How to Convert JPG to PDF Online (Step-by-Step)

  1. Visit our free image to PDF converter
  2. Click "Browse Files" or drag and drop your JPG/PNG images
  3. Select up to 20 images (they will appear as separate pages in order)
  4. Choose your PDF layout options (page size, orientation, margin)
  5. Click "Convert Now"
  6. Download your PDF instantly

Pro Tip: Arrange your images in the desired order before uploading. The tool will place each image on a separate page in the sequence you select.

Image Quality and Size Considerations

Recommended Image Settings for PDF Conversion

  • Resolution: 150-300 DPI for print-quality documents
  • Format: JPG for photographs, PNG for graphics with text
  • Color space: RGB for screens, CMYK for professional printing
  • File size: Keep individual images under 25MB for optimal performance

How to Maintain Image Quality

Our tool preserves the original image quality during conversion. The PDF will maintain the same resolution and color accuracy as your source images. For best results, use high-resolution source images (minimum 150 DPI).

Advanced PDF Layout Options

Page Size Selection

Choose from standard page sizes including A4 (210×297mm), Letter (8.5×11in), A3 (297×420mm), or let the tool automatically fit each image to its own size.

Orientation Settings

Set portrait or landscape orientation for your PDF. You can also choose "auto" to let the tool detect the optimal orientation for each image based on its dimensions.

Margin Control

Add margins around your images (small, medium, or large) to improve readability and create a more professional appearance.

Images per Page

Choose to place one image per page (default), two images per page, or create a grid layout with four images per page.
