How to Split PDF Files: Extract Specific Pages

Published: May 27, 2025 | 7 min read | By Easy-PDF-Tools Team

What is PDF Splitting?

PDF splitting is the process of extracting specific pages from a PDF document to create a new, separate PDF file. This is useful when you only need a portion of a large document, want to share certain pages without the entire file, or need to organize documents by sections.

Why Split PDF Files?

  • Share specific pages: Send only relevant pages instead of entire documents
  • Extract chapters: Separate book chapters or report sections into individual files
  • Remove unwanted pages: Create clean versions of documents without unnecessary pages
  • Organize documents: Break large PDFs into smaller, more manageable files
  • Email attachments: Reduce file size by sharing only necessary pages
  • Legal documents: Extract specific clauses or sections for review

Common Use Cases for PDF Splitting

Extracting a Single Page

Need just one page from a 100-page document? Use our split tool to extract pages 25-25, and you'll get a PDF containing only that specific page.

Extracting a Range of Pages

Extract pages 10-20 from a large report to share only the relevant section with a colleague. The tool preserves all formatting and content from those pages.

Extracting Multiple Page Ranges

Need pages 1-5 and 10-15? Use the range format "1-5,10-15" to extract multiple non-consecutive sections into a single PDF.

  1. Visit our free PDF splitter tool

Pro Tip: Use the format "1-5" to extract pages 1 through 5. Use "1,3,5" to extract specific individual pages. Use "1-5,10-15" to extract multiple ranges.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I extract pages from a scanned PDF?

Yes! Our splitter works with all PDFs, including scanned documents. The extracted pages will remain as scanned images.

What happens if I enter an invalid page number?

The tool will ignore invalid page numbers and only extract valid pages within the document's page count.

Can I split a PDF into multiple separate files?

Currently, our tool extracts pages into a single PDF. To create multiple files, use the tool multiple times with different page ranges.

Is there a limit on PDF size?

You can split PDFs up to 50MB. For larger files, please contact our support team.

Will the extracted PDF preserve bookmarks and links?

The extracted PDF will preserve all content, images, and text formatting. Links and bookmarks may not be preserved.
